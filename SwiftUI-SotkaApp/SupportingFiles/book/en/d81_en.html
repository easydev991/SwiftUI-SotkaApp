Day 81. Why your joints crackle?




Anyone who does enough time on horizontal bars and uneven bars sooner or later faces the fact that any of the joints in the body begins to crunch unpleasantly. Often this crunch does not cause pain, but it is slightly annoying. Why are joints crackling, is there any reason for concern here, what to do to protect them and why eminent chondroprotectors (glucosamine and chondroitin) do not work, we will talk about all this in today's infopost.

Joint structure




A very simplified joint can be represented as follows: two bones are connected by a movable joint, and each end of the bone is covered with hyanilin cartilage. The main task of this cartilage is to reduce friction and increase bone mobility in the joint. It has a porous structure (like a sponge) and contains a synovial (lubricating) fluid, which under stress releases into the joint. In addition, as you have already noticed, the ends of the bones are inside the joint capsule, which not only holds them together, but also produces synovial fluid.

Main causes of joint problems


1. Excess weight. An obvious factor, because the more weight, the greater the load on the joints. Moreover, both static (for example, when we are sitting at a computer) and dynamic (for example, while running or jumping).

2. Chronic infections Genitourinary or intestinal infections can cause joint problems, including arthritis. For example, simply eating a poor-quality shawarma on the street can cause problems. Any intestinal problems, acquired and congenital, can also create joint problems.

3. Autoimmune problems.

4. Hormones. Thyroid problems, such as hypothyroidism.

Why do joints crackle?


The reason is the synovial fluid mentioned above. Rather, its lack. Maybe it is produced too little, or maybe we give such big loads that the body does not have time to supply it, or maybe there are any diseases of a degenerative nature. There can be many reasons, but the result is one - there is not enough lubrication in the joint and therefore it begins to crackle and creak.

And this is much more dangerous than it might seem at first glance. In addition to the lubricating function, synovial fluid also plays the role of a nutrient medium for the joints, since the capillary system is not very developed in the joints, that is, they do not receive much nutrition from the blood.

This is not only about the amount of synovial fluid, but also about its quality (that is, the composition of the materials of which it consists).

Synovial fluid = Proteins (proteoglycans) + Carbohydrates (glycosaminoglycans) + water


For the full synthesis of glycosaminoglycans, the body requires a sufficient amount of the following three things:

1. Glucose
2. Glutamine
3. Sulfur
This, by the way, explains the fact that on various versions of keto diets, unpleasant sensations in the joints begin to appear. Because the amount of sugar in the body decreases and it becomes insufficient for the structure of the synovial fluid.

The presence of sulfur in this list should not greatly surprise you, and, moreover, you do not need to rush headlong to the nearest pharmacy to get pills. Because a sufficient amount of sulfur can be obtained from the usual diet, for example, from a plate with buckwheat. But about this, and about how to maintain joint health, we will talk further. By the way, it is sulfur that is associated with the effectiveness of the use of glucosamine-chondroitin complexes from some sports nutrition manufacturers that have the MSM component in their composition. Because this mysterious MSM is nothing but MethylSulfonylMetane, that is, the very sulfur that the body needs. And it is precisely due to this additive that the whole complex works. While chondroitin and glucosamine, as were useless, have remained, but more on that below.

Collagen for healthy joints!


When it comes to joints, in addition to talking about synovial fluid, one cannot but touch upon the issue of collagen. Of course, it is not directly related to the crunch of joints, because it is a structural protein, but since it makes up to 75% of all protein structures in our body, it is also very important. Its deficiency negatively affects not only the muscles and joints, but the sides of the skin (wrinkles), blood vessels (elasticity), intestines (gastrointestinal problems), eyes (blurred vision), etc.

For the synthesis of collagen, building materials, of course, are a necessary, but not sufficient condition. This complex multi-stage process also needs various enzymes, vitamins and minerals, i.e. a whole spectrum of different substances. In particular, the synthesis of collagen requires hydroxyproline and hydroxylysine. They are obtained from the amino acids of proline and lysine by the method of hydroxylation using vitamin C. Moreover, if the body does not have a sufficient amount of this vitamin, the hydroxylation process does not occur, the necessary substances are not synthesized, and collagen synthesis, which is so important for us, does not occur. needed.
