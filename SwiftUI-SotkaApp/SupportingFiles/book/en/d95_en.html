Day 95.

Day 95. FREESTYLE complex Chewed Mole




Enough words! Here is a new freestyle complex for you today!

After a good warm-up, perform the static Plank exercise 3 times for 30-60 seconds with rests of 10-20 seconds.

Here are some important points for you to remember:

Feet. The closer they are to each other, the more difficult it is for you to keep balance, and the higher the training load on the abdominal muscles. You can even put one on top of another or hold one leg in the air, if you are a true street workout-ninja!

Legs. Must be flat, straight and flexed!

Buttlocks. Keep them flexed during the exercise just like your legs!

Lower back. It should be flat, which means that you can not round or bent your back! Ask your friend, take a photo/video of you to see if you make mistakes! The straight back is an indicator of when it is time to finish the execution of exercise. If you feel that your back is tired and your waist falls downwards, it is time to end the exercise. Your abdominal muscles keep your back flat, and when they do not cope, the training load passes to the spine (for some time you may still be able to hold your Plank with the strength of your shoulders and buttocks, but this technique is incorrect).

Elbows. Must be strictly under the shoulder joints!

After 3 sets of Plank, you are goign to do 5 sets that will take your soul out of you and make you wish to die… Surviving this training day will provide you with grace and pacification!

Every set contains 2 exercises - a "Horseman" (familiar to you from the warm-ups of previous days), and jump squats. Think that there is nothing difficult, huh?

Set №1 - 1 minute Horseman - 10 jump squats - 1 minute Horsmen - 10 jump squats - 1 minute Horseman - 10 jump squats
Set №2 - 1 minute Horseman - 10 jump squats - 1 minute Horsmen - 10 jump squats
Set №3 - 1 minute Horseman - 10 jump squats
Set №4 - 1 minute Horseman - 10 jump squats - 1 minute Horsmen - 10 jump squats
Set №5 - 1 minute Horseman - 10 jump squats - 1 minute Horsmen - 10 jump squats - 1 minute Horseman - 10 jump squats

Rest between sets - no more than 3 minutes!

The height of the jumps depends on your physical conditioning. Obviously: the higher you jump, the harder it will be. The main thing to remember is a soft landing after each jump!

That's all for today, was it as easy as it sounded in the beginning? We hope not! Anyway, a new TURBO program will be waiting for you tomorrow!
