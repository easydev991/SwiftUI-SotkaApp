Carbohydrates




Carbohydrates is the main source of energy in the body, and at the same time one of the basic sources of the overweight problems.

3 important functions of carbohydrates


1. Act as a source of energy for the body.
2. Participate in the process of cells and tissues formation.
3. They are part of the protoplasm, subcellular and cellular structures and participate in the vital activity of cells.

Carbohydrates provide all cells and living tissues of the body with energy. However, the Central Nervous System needs them most. Our brain consumes about 100 grams of pure glucose per day (380 kcal)

Approximately the same amount of carbohydrates is the minimum, which is required per day to provide energy to tissues that process glucose. And the same amount is needed to reduce the probability that, as a lack of carbohydrates, the body will begin to provide itself with energy splitting proteins. It is also called protein-saving carbohydrate effect.

Types of carbohydrates


Carbohydrates can be simple and complex, depending on whether they consist of single molecules or their chains.

Simple carbohydrates are used mainly for direct energy supply, but they can also be stored as complex polysaccharides. For plants it is starch, and for animals and humans there is glycogen, which is deposited in the liver and muscles.

Exactly the glycogen, stored directly in the muscles, is used in strength trainings for their energy supply. It is very important to control the presence of a sufficient amount of carbs in your diet. Otherwise, with a chronic shortage of carbohydrates in the diet, glycogen stores will gradually decline, which can lead to the accumulation of chronic fatigue.

Complex carbohydrates are digested slowly, they gradually enter the blood, smoothly maintaining the level of glucose in it. They supply the body with energy for a long time. For example, carbohydrates contained in pasta can being absorbed up to 12 hours. Thus, if you choose mainly complex carbohydrates for your meal, the feeling of satiety will last much longer, the appetite bursts will become fewer, and the desire to eat something sweet will disappear.

Simple carbohydrates cause a sharp rise in the level of glucose in the blood. Complex carbohydrates increase blood sugar not so intensive, but for a longer time.
