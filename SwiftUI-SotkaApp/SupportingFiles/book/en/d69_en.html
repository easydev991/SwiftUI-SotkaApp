Day 69. Bodyweight Exercises Database




While we have started this week talking about new exercises, it would be logic to end this week with one big infopost in which we will systemize different popular exercises into groups, depending on muscles involved and their difficulty level.

IMPORTANT!!!
This information is given for consideration only and for your overall awareness (don’t forget we are having an education program). We recommend using it AFTER finishing our 100 Days Workout programm.


Classification basis


Today, there is a huge amount of bodyweight exercises, but Street Workout is about people using their imagination and coming up with something new. And this is great! The problems start when you try to structurize everything in one unified system.

We’ve chosen the simplest path and identified two main characteristics of any exercise: main muscle group and exertion level.

Exercise type - there are a lot of different bodyweight exercises, but still they can be referred to one of several basic types which are 6 with us::

1. Push-ups
2. Pull-ups
3. Dips
4. Muscle-ups
5. Muscle-Up
6. ABS Exercises
Many exercises involve several muscle groups, therefore these exercises are efficient. We decided to classify them by type rather than by trained muscles.

Exertion level - this parameter demonstrates the exertion experienced by the muscle in this or that exercise. This level is based on biomechanics and physics, movement amplitude, weight, the amount of involved muscles, etc. There are advanced and easy exercises, we’ve tried to divide the exercises by levels.

I level - beginners (roughly), lead-up exercises, that is, simplified versions of the exercises which are easier to perform than the exercise itself and which can give you an opportunity to acquire new strength. And their modifications.

II level - advanced trainees (roughly), the exercise itself. And its different modifications.

III level - elite athletes (roughly), more advanced modifications of the exercise which give more exertion for the same time period. You can do this exercise to put the muscles under stress and to shorten a session time.

The exercises are randomly organized within one particular level.
